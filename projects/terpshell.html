The Specs

Intel I7 4770K running at stock speed, and I will probably keep it that way to extend the useful life of the processor. For cooling I went with a prefilled water cooling solution, the Cooler Master Seidon 240M. The average idle temperature dropped from 34 degrees celcius with the stock cooler to 24 degrees celcius with the Cooler Master.

For the case I went with the Corsair 350D, a micro ATX case with enough room for several radiators if I ever feel the need to custom water cool my build. I went with the Corsair C600M for power, and use a custom built cover to hide the wires. I highly recommend Corsair products, especially their cases. They have superior build quality to the other cases I've worked with, but they also have good routing holes and wire management leads to a clean looking build.

For memory I went with 2x8 GBs of Corsair Vengance RAM. I do a lot of multi tasking (Photoshop alongside Solidworks for instance) and the 16 GBs are the perfect amount of RAM for me, and I have 2 slots left to expand up to 32 GBs on my Gigabyte motherboard.

I have a Samsung 840 SSD for my OS and a WD Blue 1 TB drive for general storage. I will be replacing my OS drive with a Samsung EVO and moving the SSD into my laptop or a new build.
